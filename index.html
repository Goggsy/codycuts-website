Questions

Good to Know

How often should my lawn be cut?

Through the growing season, every two to three weeks keeps a lawn healthy and easy to manage. Leave it much longer and the next cut becomes a bigger job — which is why first cuts on overgrown lawns are priced differently.

Why do you need photos before quoting?

Size, grass length and access all change how long a job takes. Photos let Cody give you an accurate price up front rather than a vague estimate that changes on the day.

What happens to the grass cuttings?

Three options, all included: we take the cuttings away, we mulch them back into the lawn as a natural feed, or we put them in your green waste bin. Just tell us which you'd prefer.

Do I need to be home?

Not necessarily. As long as we have access to the garden, Cody can carry out the work and let you know when it's done.

Do you cut commercial or communal lawns?

Cody Cuts focuses on residential gardens. If you have something larger in mind, get in touch and we'll be straight with you about whether it's a good fit.

How old is Cody?

Cody is fourteen and runs the business himself. His father attends every job with him — both to assist and to make sure the standard of work never slips.
